Information We Collect

Personal Information You Provide

When you register for and use HomePlates, we collect:

  • Identity Data: Full name, username, date of birth
  • Contact Data: Email address, phone number, delivery address
  • Profile Data: Profile photo (optional), dietary preferences, allergen information
  • Financial Data: Payment card details, billing address (processed securely via our payment processors — we do not store full card numbers)
  • Transaction Data: Order history, payment amounts, wallet balances
  • Communication Data: Messages exchanged with chefs or riders via in-app chat, support tickets
  • User Content: Reviews, ratings, and uploaded food photos

Location Data

With your explicit consent, we collect precise geolocation data to:

  • Match you with nearby chefs and riders
  • Enable real-time order delivery tracking
  • Show relevant dishes available in your area
  • Calculate accurate delivery estimates and fees

You can revoke location permissions at any time through your device settings. Without location access, certain features (such as delivery tracking) may be unavailable.

Automatically Collected Data

When you use the Service, we automatically collect:

  • Device Data: Device model, operating system and version, unique device identifiers, screen resolution
  • Usage Data: Features used, pages visited, session duration, interaction patterns
  • Log Data: IP address, access times, app crash reports, referring URLs
  • Cookies & Similar Technologies: We use local storage tokens for session management and analytics identifiers (see our Cookie Policy for details)

Data from Third Parties

We may receive data about you from:

  • Social login providers (e.g., Google, Apple) if you choose to sign in with a social account — limited to your name, email, and profile picture

Data Retention

We retain your personal data only for as long as necessary to fulfil the purposes described in this policy, or as required by law:

  • Account Data: Retained for the duration of your account plus 30 days after deletion to allow for account recovery
  • Transaction Records: Retained for 7 years to comply with financial and tax regulations
  • Support Tickets: Retained for 3 years after resolution
  • Location Data: Deleted within 90 days of collection; aggregated (non-personal) analytics may be retained longer
  • Marketing Preferences: Retained until you withdraw consent
  • Log & Device Data: Retained for up to 12 months

When data is no longer needed, it is securely deleted or anonymised so it can no longer be associated with you.

Data Sharing

We share your data only when necessary and only with the following categories of recipients:

  • Order Fulfilment: Your name, delivery address, and order details are shared with the assigned chef and rider to facilitate delivery
  • Payment Processors: Financial data is securely transmitted to Stripe and local payment gateways for transaction processing
  • Cloud Infrastructure: Account and order data is stored on Supabase (hosted in the EU region) and utilises PostgreSQL with Row Level Security
  • Mapping Services: Your location data is sent to Google Maps Platform to enable delivery tracking and address autocomplete
  • Analytics Providers: Anonymised usage data may be shared with analytics services to help us improve the app
  • Law Enforcement: When required by law, court order, or to protect the rights, property, or safety of HomePlates, our users, or the public
  • Business Transfers: In the event of a merger, acquisition, or sale of assets, your data may be transferred as part of the transaction — you will be notified in advance
We never sell your personal information to third parties.

International Data Transfers

Your personal data may be transferred to, and processed in, countries outside your country of residence. Where we transfer data outside the UK/EEA, we ensure appropriate safeguards are in place, including:

  • Adequacy Decisions: Transfers to countries deemed adequate by the European Commission or the UK Secretary of State
  • Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs): EU-approved contractual clauses with our service providers
  • Supplementary Measures: Additional technical and organisational security measures where required

You may obtain a copy of the safeguards we use by contacting our DPO at dpo@homeplates.app.

Data Security

We implement robust technical and organisational measures to protect your data:

  • Encryption in Transit: All data transmitted between the app and our servers is encrypted using HTTPS/TLS 1.3
  • Encryption at Rest: Sensitive data stored on your device uses Hive AES-256 encryption; server-side data is encrypted at rest
  • Access Controls: Supabase Row Level Security (RLS) ensures users can only access their own data; staff access is role-based and logged
  • Authentication: Passwords are hashed using bcrypt; multi-factor authentication is available
  • Regular Audits: We conduct periodic security audits and vulnerability assessments
  • Breach Response: We maintain an incident response plan and will notify affected users and the relevant supervisory authority within 72 hours of a confirmed breach, as required by GDPR Article 33

Your Rights

Under the GDPR and applicable data protection laws, you have the following rights regarding your personal data:

Right of Access (Art. 15)

You have the right to obtain confirmation of whether we process your personal data and to receive a copy of that data in a commonly used, machine-readable format.

Right to Rectification (Art. 16)

You can request correction of inaccurate personal data or completion of incomplete data. Most profile information can be updated directly in the app under Settings > Profile.

Right to Erasure / "Right to be Forgotten" (Art. 17)

You can request deletion of your personal data. We will comply unless we have a legal obligation to retain certain data (e.g., financial records for tax compliance).

Right to Restriction of Processing (Art. 18)

You can request that we limit the processing of your data while a dispute is being resolved or when you contest the accuracy of your data.

Right to Data Portability (Art. 20)

You can request your data in a structured, commonly used, machine-readable format (JSON or CSV) and have it transmitted to another controller. You can initiate an export from Settings > Privacy > Export My Data.

Right to Object (Art. 21)

You can object to processing based on legitimate interests, including profiling. You can also object to the use of your data for direct marketing at any time, with no conditions.

Right to Withdraw Consent (Art. 7(3))

Where processing is based on consent, you may withdraw your consent at any time without affecting the lawfulness of processing carried out before withdrawal.

Right Not to be Subject to Automated Decision-Making (Art. 22)

You have the right not to be subject to a decision based solely on automated processing, including profiling, which produces legal or similarly significant effects. HomePlates does not currently use fully automated decision-making that affects your rights.

Right to Lodge a Complaint

You have the right to lodge a complaint with a supervisory authority. In the UK, this is the Information Commissioner's Office (ICO). In the EU, you may contact your local Data Protection Authority.

How to Exercise Your Rights

You can exercise any of these rights by:

  • In-App: Navigate to Settings > Privacy > Manage My Data for self-service options including data export and account deletion
  • Email: Send a request to dpo@homeplates.app with the subject line "Data Rights Request"
  • Post: Write to our DPO at the address listed above

Response Time: We will acknowledge your request within 48 hours and respond substantively within 30 days. If the request is complex, we may extend this by up to two additional months and will inform you of the reason for the delay.

Verification: To protect your privacy, we will verify your identity before processing a request. This may involve confirming your email address or asking security questions associated with your account.

Cost: Exercising your rights is free of charge. If requests are manifestly unfounded or excessive, we may charge a reasonable fee or refuse to act, providing reasons.
